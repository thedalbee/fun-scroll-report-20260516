# Theory notes

## Humor / fun mechanisms gathered before 20:30
- Incongruity theory: humor often starts when expectation and outcome do not match. For video, this means the viewer predicts A, image does B.
- Benign violation theory: funny when a norm/order is violated but the violation is safe enough to enjoy. Too harmless = boring; too threatening = discomfort; sweet spot = wrong but safe.
- Curiosity/reward: information gaps can act like reward loops; the mind wants closure when the scene implies “something doesn’t add up.”
- Flow/game-design: fun is not only winning. It comes from challenge, progress, feedback, agency, micro-achievement, and “see what happens next.”
- Reddit r/gamedesign community signal: people discuss fun through reward loops, satisfying wins, challenge vs annoyance, and intrinsic progress rather than prize alone.

## 20:42 Theory cross-check
- Humor theories cluster around incongruity, relief/tension release, superiority/status, and benign violation. Strongest applied short-form model: create tension/incongruity, keep it safe, then resolve or escalate.
- Flow: fun requires energized focus and balance of challenge vs skill. For passive video, this maps to “viewer can almost solve it” — not too obvious, not too confusing.
- Self-determination: intrinsic motivation is supported by autonomy, competence, relatedness. For community content: let viewer feel “I get this,” “I chose to stay,” “people like me understand this.”
- Combined content formula: safe wrongness + solvable gap + social recognition + quick feedback/payoff.

## 20:44 Visual attention science cross-check
- Visual attention is guided by bottom-up factors (color/luminance/brightness/saturation contrast), top-down factors (task, motivation, emotion), and spatial biases.
- Natural images can have “global salience” that predicts first fixation beyond simple local contrast. For shorts this means: not just neon text; the whole composition must make one target obviously worth looking at.
- Novelty/surprise competes with raw salience. The strongest first frame combines both: high contrast + semantic wrongness.
